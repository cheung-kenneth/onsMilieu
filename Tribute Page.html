Umbrella Movement

The Umbrella Movement (Chinese: 雨傘運動) is a pro-democracy political movement that was created spontaneously during the Hong Kong protests of 2014. Its name derives from the recognition of the umbrella as a symbol of defiance and resistance against the Hong Kong Police, and the united grass-roots objection to the decision of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress (NPCSC) of 31 August.

August 31, 2014
Terms are set for the 2017 Elections from the National People's Congress Standing Committee.

Because candidates whom Beijing deems unsuitable would not be nominated, pro-democracy advocates view the decision as a betrayal of the principle of "one person, one vote."

September 22, 2014
Two student movements, the Hong Kong Federation of Students (HKFS) and “Scholarism” begin a class boycott.

Their demands include: universal suffrage, the resignation of Chief Executive Leung Chun-ying, the withdrawal of the NPC Standing Committee’s decision, and the submission of a new electoral reform plan.

September 26, 2014
The police arrest Joshua Wong, a veteran activist at the age of only 17 and the organizer of Scholarism, but release him two days later on Sept. 28.

September 27, 2014
Protesters march to central government offices. Police use pepper spray against the protesters. Several protesters are injured and more are detained.
